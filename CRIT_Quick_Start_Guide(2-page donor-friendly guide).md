# CRIT Quick Start Guide (Pilot Implementation)

## What is CRIT?
The **Climate-Resilient School Infrastructure Toolkit (CRIT)** is an open-source toolkit developed by **WUM Advisory Services** to strengthen school infrastructure resilience against climate hazards such as floods, heatwaves, storms, and extreme rainfall.

CRIT enables education authorities, engineers, and implementing partners to assess school vulnerability, prioritize high-risk schools, implement modular retrofitting solutions, and track resilience outcomes.

---

## Why CRIT Matters
Climate shocks increasingly disrupt education continuity by damaging schools and forcing closures. Public school infrastructure in Pakistan is highly exposed due to weak drainage systems, poor heat mitigation, limited disaster preparedness features, and constrained technical planning capacity.

CRIT addresses these gaps by offering standardized yet adaptable tools aligned with safe schools principles and climate adaptation priorities.

---

# How to Use CRIT (6 Steps)

## Step 1: Select Target Districts
Use hazard exposure evidence and education vulnerability indicators to select high-risk districts. Priority should be given to underserved communities with limited alternative schooling options.

## Step 2: Assess Schools (Module 01)
Apply the CRIT climate risk screening tool and vulnerability checklist. Assign hazard, infrastructure, and social vulnerability scores to calculate a composite risk score.

## Step 3: Prioritize Schools
Rank schools based on risk score and urgency. Identify schools requiring immediate safety repairs versus planned retrofitting.

## Step 4: Select Retrofitting Packages (Module 03)
Choose modular interventions based on risk profile:
- flood protection and drainage upgrades  
- cool roofs and ventilation improvements  
- WASH resilience upgrades  
- storm strengthening and roof anchoring  
- electrical safety improvements  
- inclusive accessibility upgrades  

## Step 5: Cost and Plan Implementation (Module 04)
Use CRIT costing calculators and BOQ templates to estimate costs and prepare procurement plans. Include supervision and contingency.

## Step 6: Implement and Monitor (Module 06)
Conduct retrofitting works under quality assurance procedures. Establish baseline and endline assessments to measure improvements. Track outputs, outcomes, and learning continuity indicators.

---

# Pilot Outputs (Suggested Minimum)
- 10–20 schools assessed and ranked  
- 10–15 schools retrofitted using CRIT packages  
- 50+ district engineers/planners trained  
- baseline and endline resilience scores recorded  
- documented case studies and cost-benefit evidence  

---

# Key Indicators for Results Tracking
- reduction in climate-related closure days  
- improved structural safety score  
- improved thermal comfort indicators  
- improved WASH continuity during shocks  
- increased community participation in maintenance  

---

# Sustainability Strategy
CRIT supports sustainability through:
- integration into district planning cycles  
- capacity building of local technical teams  
- community ownership through School Management Committees  
- preventive maintenance planning and monitoring  

---

# Equity and Inclusion
CRIT prioritizes vulnerable communities and integrates:
- gender-responsive WASH and privacy  
- disability-accessible infrastructure  
- equitable selection criteria focused on marginalized districts
