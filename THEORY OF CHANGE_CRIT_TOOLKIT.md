CLIMATE-RESILIENT SCHOOL INFRASTRUCTURE TOOLKIT (CRIT)
THEORY OF CHANGE

------------------------------------------------------------
1. DEVELOPMENT CHALLENGE
------------------------------------------------------------
Climate change is increasingly disrupting education systems in Pakistan through floods, heatwaves, storms, and water scarcity. School infrastructure is highly vulnerable due to weak design standards, limited climate risk integration, and insufficient maintenance systems.

This results in:
- Frequent school closures
- Infrastructure damage and high repair costs
- Unsafe learning environments
- Disproportionate impact on girls and vulnerable children
- Reduced learning continuity and increased dropout risk

------------------------------------------------------------
2. IMPACT STATEMENT (LONG-TERM GOAL)
------------------------------------------------------------
Children in climate-vulnerable regions of Pakistan access safe, resilient, and uninterrupted learning environments despite increasing climate shocks.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. OUTCOME PATHWAYS
------------------------------------------------------------

OUTCOME 1: Improved climate resilience of school infrastructure
- Schools are structurally safer and climate-adapted
- Reduced damage from floods, heat, and storms
- Improved infrastructure safety scores

OUTCOME 2: Improved continuity of learning during climate shocks
- Reduced school closure days
- Increased attendance during extreme weather events
- Reduced loss of learning materials and teaching time

OUTCOME 3: Strengthened system capacity for climate-resilient planning
- District engineers and planners trained in CRIT tools
- Climate-risk integrated into education infrastructure planning
- Standardized assessment and decision-making tools institutionalized

------------------------------------------------------------
4. OUTPUTS
------------------------------------------------------------
- Schools assessed using standardized CRIT risk tools
- Schools prioritized based on composite climate risk scores
- Modular retrofitting packages implemented
- Climate-resilient design standards applied
- Capacity building programs delivered
- Monitoring and evaluation system operational

------------------------------------------------------------
5. ACTIVITIES
------------------------------------------------------------
- Climate risk screening and vulnerability assessments
- School prioritization using scoring framework
- Design and implementation of retrofit packages:
  * Flood protection systems
  * Heat mitigation solutions
  * Storm resilience strengthening
  * WASH system upgrades
  * Accessibility and inclusion improvements
- Training of engineers, planners, and school staff
- Monitoring, evaluation, and learning cycles
- Community engagement through School Management Committees

------------------------------------------------------------
6. ASSUMPTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
- Government collaboration at provincial and district levels
- Availability of basic construction resources
- Community participation in maintenance systems
- Stable implementation environment during pilot phase

------------------------------------------------------------
7. RISKS AND MITIGATION
------------------------------------------------------------
Risk: Procurement delays
Mitigation: Pre-qualified vendors and modular procurement

Risk: Poor construction quality
Mitigation: Technical supervision and QA checklists

Risk: Limited adoption by institutions
Mitigation: Capacity building + government integration

Risk: Climate shocks during implementation
Mitigation: Adaptive scheduling and emergency response protocols

------------------------------------------------------------
