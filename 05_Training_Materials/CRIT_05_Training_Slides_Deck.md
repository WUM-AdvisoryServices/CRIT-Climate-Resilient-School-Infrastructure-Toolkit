# Slide 3: Why Climate-Resilient Schools Matter
Climate shocks increasingly disrupt education systems through:
- Flood damage and waterlogging  
- Heatwaves affecting classroom safety and attendance  
- Storms causing structural damage  
- WASH system breakdowns during emergencies  

**Result:** school closures, learning loss, increased dropout risk, higher reconstruction costs.  

---

# Slide 4: Pakistan Context – Key Risks
Major infrastructure threats include:
- Monsoon flooding (river and flash floods)  
- Extreme heat and urban heat stress  
- Poor drainage and waterlogging  
- Weak roofing and boundary structures  
- Limited climate-resilient design standards in public schools  

**Most affected:** rural, low-income, and disaster-prone districts.

# Slide 6: What is CRIT?
CRIT is an open-source toolkit providing:
- Climate risk screening tools  
- Vulnerability scoring templates  
- Modular design standards  
- Retrofitting guidance  
- Costing and BOQ templates  
- Implementation SOPs  
- Monitoring & evaluation indicators  
- Community engagement tools  

**Goal:** ensure safe learning continuity during climate shocks.  

---

# Slide 7: CRIT Modules Overview
CRIT consists of 7 modules:
1. Risk Assessment & Prioritization  
2. Climate-Resilient Design Templates  
3. Retrofitting Guidelines  
4. Costing Tools & Cost-Benefit Planning  
5. Training & Capacity Building  
6. Pilot Implementation & Governance  
7. Sample Data & Demonstration Resources  

---

# Slide 8: CRIT Implementation Flow
CRIT follows a structured process:
1. Identify hazard exposure areas  
2. Assess schools using CRIT tool  
3. Score and rank risk levels  
4. Select retrofit package(s)  
5. Prepare BOQ and budget  
6. Implement with quality monitoring  
7. Engage community and ensure maintenance  
8. Track outcomes and learning continuity  

---

# Slide 9: Module 01 – Risk Screening
Module 01 helps answer:
- Which schools are at highest risk?  
- Which hazards are most dangerous?  
- Which infrastructure elements are weakest?  
- Which communities are most vulnerable?  

**Outputs:**
- Composite risk score  
- Risk classification (Low–Critical)  
- Priority ranking list  

---

# Slide 10: Hazard Exposure Scoring (0–3)
Hazards assessed include:
- Flooding  
- Waterlogging  
- Heatwaves  
- Storms / high winds  
- Drought / water scarcity  
- Landslides (if applicable)  
- Earthquake risk (if applicable)  

**Score Meaning:**  
0 = no exposure  
1 = low  
2 = medium  
3 = high  

---

# Slide 11: Infrastructure Vulnerability Scoring
Infrastructure components scored include:
- Foundation/plinth height  
- Roof condition  
- Wall cracks/stability  
- Drainage system  
- Ventilation and heat protection  
- WASH resilience  
- Electrical safety  
- Boundary wall safety  
- Emergency exits / assembly areas  

---

# Slide 12: Social Vulnerability Scoring
Social risk indicators include:
- Poverty vulnerability  
- Remoteness / no alternative schools  
- Girls’ dependency on local school  
- Disability access barriers  
- Dropout history during shocks  
- Weak coping capacity  

**Equity lens is mandatory.**  

---

# Slide 13: Composite Risk Score
**Composite Risk Score = Hazard Exposure + Infrastructure Vulnerability + Social Vulnerability**  

**Risk Classification:**  
- 0–10 = Low  
- 11–20 = Medium  
- 21–30 = High  
- 31+ = Critical

---

# Slide 16: Flood-Resilient Design Features
Key design features:
- Raised plinth above flood line  
- Perimeter drainage and slope control  
- Water-resistant flooring  
- Elevated storage for learning materials  
- Safe access routes during flood season  
- Strengthened boundary walls where needed  

---

# Slide 17: Heat Mitigation Design Features
Heat-resilient schools should include:
- Cross ventilation  
- High ceilings for airflow  
- Reflective cool roofs  
- Veranda shading and overhangs  
- Shade trees and courtyard design  
- Hydration and drinking water points  

---

# Slide 18: Storm and Wind Resilience
Storm safety improvements include:
- Roof anchoring reinforcement  
- Strong truss systems  
- Safe windows and doors  
- Removal of unsafe external structures  
- Protective boundary design  

---

# Slide 19: WASH Resilience and Safety
Resilient WASH systems include:
- Flood-protected toilets  
- Gender-segregated toilets with privacy  
- Handwashing stations with drainage  
- Water storage for drought/heat  
- Accessible toilets for children with disabilities  

---

# Slide 20: Inclusion and Accessibility
Minimum inclusive standards:
- Ramps to classrooms  
- Safe pathways and handrails  
- Accessible toilets  
- Privacy for girls  
- Lighting and safety signage  

**CRIT prioritizes equity and child rights.**
